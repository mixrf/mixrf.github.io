Abstract & Method

Neural Radiance Fields (NeRFs) have recently attracted significant attention due to their advanced capability in photorealistic view synthesis. However, NeRF-based methods decompose the scene into a 5D continuous radiance field consisting of independently sampled spatial points along individual rays. This independence lacks the modeling capacity for local smoothness within the color domain and spatial dependencies, resulting in an underutilization of spatial relationships in the scene. This paper specifically addresses NeRF-based methods based on dense sampling and proposes a novel and efficient method for attribute mixing in both radiance field and color domain, termed MixNeRF. Our method supplements the missing structural modeling capabilities and smoothness in synthesized views of NeRFs, significantly enhancing the view synthesis performance. We employ learnable dynamic kernel strategies to perform global radiance field mixing and local pixel mixing in both the radiance field space and color domain. Specifically, we globally blend colors and volume densities, respectively, in the radiance field space, resulting in the target pixel values after volume rendering. Then, we perform local blending in the color domain on the synthesized pixel patch to increase their smoothness, introducing Plücker coordinates as view features to ensure view consistency. Through extensive qualitative and quantitative analysis across synthetic and real datasets, our method demonstrates substantial performance improvements for NeRF-based methods.


FLIP Comparison

We use the FLIP error estimator to produce error maps that model how likely humans would perceive errors when "flipping" between an image and the target output. DONeRF shows similar or better results at significantly lower performance requirements.
